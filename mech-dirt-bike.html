Team Eligibility Criteria
• All members of the participating team should have a valid college ID card and should produce it at the time of registration. No team member will be allowed to participate without college ID card.
• The teams can have names of their choice and should have a team logo.
• A maximum of 10 team members are only allowed to participate and they are only eligible for getting certificates. The team mates can be from the same college or different colleges/university.
Bike Specifications
• The bikes engine capacity should be between 60cc to 80cc.
• The bike can be of any dimensions.
• Inspection of vehicle will be done before the commencement of the event.
• Respective teams should bring fuel and tool kit.
Registration
• The total registration fee for a team is Rs. 3500/- and advance amount of Rs.1500/- should be paid prior to the event for the confirmation of the team. (NOTE: advance amount should be paid through bank and paid receipt or Challan should be mailed with team details)
• The team will have to register first in the official website WWW.DRESTEIN.IN.
Driver Eligibility Criteria
• It is compulsory that two drivers must enrol per team.
• The driver must be minimum 18 years old.
• The driver must have a valid driver’s license and should produce it when demanded during the competition.
• The driver should possess a valid health insurance and should produce a photocopy of it during the event.
• Riding gears are compulsory for the driver (Helmet, Gloves, Shoes, Kneepad). No rider is allowed to enter into the track without the above mentioned safety gear.
• The riders should submit a declaration form duly signed by their parents along with the college seal
Task
• The teams will have to compete in two rounds, technical inspection and On track race event.
Technical Inspection
A clear document must be submitted both as a soft copy and hard copy requiring all the specifications of the bike including the entire cost.
The bike will be assessed by a technical committee and will be checked if the bike complies by the rules specified by the organizing committee and also the documentation submitted by the teams.
The procedure followed for the technical inspection is entirely at the diplomacy of the technical committee.
The cost of the bike specified by the teams in the documentation will be cross checked by the technical committee and if any variation is found the team stands a chance of losing points.
It is expected of every team to include some sort of innovation in their bikes and the points are also allotted for innovation.
The teams will have to answer the questions put up by the technical committee.
The allocation of points to the teams lies solely in the hands of technical committee and their decision is final.

On Track Events
This consists of three rounds, break test, Skid pad and race events.
Break Test
Before the teams enter the racing arena, all the bikes have to pass the break test. The bike must stop in between two marked points after the break is applied.
Skid pad The bikes will have to clear the 8 shaped track without the rider placing his legs on the ground. For every time the rider places his legs on the ground while driving the bike the team loses 10 points from the allocated points for skid pad.
Race events
This event is separated into three rounds
First round
The bikes after completing the break test and skid pad will be allowed to enter in to the race arena. In the first round the teams will be allowed to ride for 2 laps and the lap timing of the respective teams will be noted down. The top four teams with best lap timing alone will progress to the next round.
NOTE:
1. The timer will be stopped every time the bike engine stops and will start only when the bike starts.
2. The track will have obstacles at regular intervals. If a bike tumbles or gets struck at any point on the track, our volunteers will help the driver in continuing the race. The time shall be running in the meantime.
3. In all the rounds only the driver is allowed to enter into the racing arena.
4. The bikes are not allowed to leave any loose parts on the track.
Second round
The top four teams will be grouped into two by the organizing panel and the teams have to compete against each other in their respective groups and the top two teams will propel to the final round.
Third round
The final round will be a head on head racing of two laps and the one who completes first is the winner of the race events.
(NOTE: Head on head will be like each rider riding in opposite direction. One will be riding in forward track another in reverse track for the 1st lap and vice versa for the 2nd lap)
NOTE:
1. Teams are not allowed to purposefully damage the bike of the opponent’s team. If found guilty for doing so on the track the concerned team will be disqualified from the race.

Event rules and regulations
• If the bike is damaged during the race, the team is allowed to rectify the problem and start again from the starting line.
• If any team is found not abiding by the rules, then the respective team will be eliminated from the event.
• The decision made by the judges and the organising committee is final; any further arguments by the participants will lead to the termination of the entire team from the event.
• Tobacco products and alcohol is strictly banned inside the college premises. If any team is found using or in possession of the banned products, the team will be expelled from the event immediately.
• The registration amount will not be refunded to the team.
• Judge’s decision will be treated as final and binding on all.
• The organizers reserve the rights to change any or all of the above rules as they deem fit. Change in rules, if any will be highlighted on the website and notified to the registered teams through E-mail or by phone.
